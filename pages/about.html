Our Story

Why RILOC Exists

RILOC was founded on a simple observation: the organizations best equipped to support refugee and immigrant communities are the ones built by refugees and immigrants themselves. Too many newcomers were navigating housing, jobs, and healthcare systems alone — often through services that didn't reflect their language, culture, or lived experience.

We formed as a coalition to change that: pooling resources, expertise, and advocacy power across refugee and immigrant-led organizations so that no single group has to face systemic barriers alone. Today, RILOC connects a growing network of community partners delivering coordinated, culturally responsive programs across housing, education, workforce development, health, and civic life.

Our Mission

RILOC is a nonprofit community consortium dedicated to empowering refugees, immigrants, and underserved populations through economic empowerment, advocacy, leadership development, and community integration.

Our Vision

To create thriving, inclusive, and economically empowered communities where refugees and immigrants achieve long-term success, full civic participation, and lasting belonging.

Our Philosophy

Organizational Objectives

RILOC organizes its work around clear, measurable objectives that keep every program accountable to the communities it serves.

  • Expand access to economic opportunity for refugee and immigrant households.
  • Strengthen civic participation and community leadership pipelines.
  • Deliver culturally responsive health, wellness, and family support services.
  • Advocate for policy that protects and advances immigrant rights.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can't find what you're looking for? Contact our team.

Who is eligible for RILOC programs?

Our programs primarily serve refugees, immigrants, and underserved community members, though many workshops are open to the broader public. Eligibility details are listed on each program page.

Is RILOC affiliated with a specific country or region?

No. RILOC is a coalition of refugee and immigrant-led organizations serving newcomers from many countries and backgrounds.

How is RILOC funded?

Through a mix of individual donations, foundation grants, government contracts, and corporate partnerships — all detailed in our annual report.

Can my organization become a coalition partner?

Yes. Visit our Partners page to learn about NGO, business, government, and university partnership tracks.
